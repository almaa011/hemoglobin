01 Key numbers

What you're working with

A reflectance PPG signal sits on top of a DC pedestal 100–1000× larger than the AC component you care about. The numbers below define the dynamic range problem.[1]

1–2%
AC/DC ratio — pulsatile vs. static perfusion
530 nm
Peak green wavelength — highest AC amplitude, most motion noise
660 / 940 nm
Red / IR pair for SpO₂ — smaller AC, less motion sensitivity
18–20 bit
Effective ADC resolution needed to resolve the AC component
The DC pedestal comes from venous blood, tissue, and bone — it does not pulse. You're trying to resolve the ~1% that does.

02 Signal chain

LED → photodiode → TIA → ADC

The signal chain has four stages. Each introduces a constraint. Understanding the constraint at each stage tells you where your noise budget is going.[2]

End-to-end signal chain
reflectance PPG · wrist

Every stage trades something. The TIA sets noise floor. The ADC sets resolution. The LED driver sets power and ambient rejection margin.

Stage 1 — LED driver

Drive current sets photon flux, which sets the DC photocurrent at the detector. More current → more signal → better SNR, at the cost of power and self-heating. Typical range: 6–50 mA pulsed at 25–50% duty cycle.[1]

Stage 2 — Photodiode + TIA

The photodiode outputs a current proportional to irradiance. A transimpedance amplifier converts that to voltage. The TIA feedback resistor Rf sets gain and bandwidth: V_out = I_pd × R_f. Shot noise from the DC photocurrent is typically the dominant noise source, not op-amp input noise.[2]

Formula / definition

Shot noise current: in = √(2 · q · IDC · BW) — where q = 1.6×10⁻¹⁹ C, IDC is the DC photocurrent in amps, and BW is the measurement bandwidth in Hz. At IDC = 10 µA and BW = 100 Hz, in ≈ 18 pArms.

Key finding

Shot noise from the DC photocurrent sets a hard floor that cannot be improved by better op-amp selection. The only lever is to reduce DC photocurrent while maintaining AC signal — which means reducing ambient light, not LED drive current.[2]

Warning

Ambient fluorescent lighting at 100–120 Hz aliases directly into the PPG band. Without synchronous detection (LED on/off subtraction), a window next to a fluorescent lamp will saturate your TIA before you pick up a heartbeat.[3]

Conflict surfaced — green vs. red for motion noise

Researcher A (primary sources) states green has higher AC amplitude and better SNR at rest. Researcher C (adversarial) notes that green's higher melanin absorption makes it significantly more motion-sensitive on darker skin tones than red/IR. Both are correct in different conditions — choose wavelength based on use-case and target population.

04 Component comparison

Integrated AFE options

Dedicated PPG AFEs combine LED driver, TIA, ADC, and ambient cancellation in one IC. Key trade-offs across three common parts.[4]

Part ADC res. LED channels Ambient cancel I²C / SPI Fit
MAX30102 18-bit Red + IR Basic I²C SpO₂ only, no green
AFE4490 22-bit Red + IR Synchronous subtraction SPI Medical-grade SpO₂
MAX86916 19-bit R + IR + G + B 4-slot sequencing I²C Wearable HR + SpO₂
SFH7060 N/A (analog out) R + IR + G None — Needs external AFE
The AFE4490 datasheet specifies 107 dB of dynamic range — enough to resolve 1% AC on top of a full-scale DC current without external signal conditioning.[4]
