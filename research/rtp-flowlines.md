## ⚠️ CONFLICTS BETWEEN RESEARCHERS / FLAGGED ITEMS

1. **Spool lengths**: Researcher A states RTP typical spool length = 300 m; Researcher B states spoolable up to 400 m per reel. TCP manufacturers claim continuous lengths up to 6 km. The 300 m vs 400 m discrepancy likely reflects different product families or diameter ranges — both values are cited from sources but may not be directly comparable.

2. **API 15S vs DNV-ST-F119**: These two major standards have documented different long-term pressure strength qualification requirements. Engineers must establish which standard governs their project jurisdiction before qualification testing.

3. **ISO 23936-2 sufficiency**: One source confirms ISO 23936-2 acceptance criteria are "not suitable for actual seal pressure validation" — this is a documented limitation of the standard, not a fringe finding. Operators should confirm whether their seal qualification goes beyond the minimum ISO test parameters.

4. **Field conditions vs. qualification envelope**: Field pressures up to 600 barg and 200°C exceed typical RTP ratings (≤450 bar, ≤121°C for TCP; less for unbonded RTP). This means RTP is not a direct steel replacement for high-pressure sour gas service — it occupies a specific operating window.
